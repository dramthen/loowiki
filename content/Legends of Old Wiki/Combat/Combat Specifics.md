Combat in Legends of Old is similar to MMOs of yester-year, with far less downtime than them.

**Targeting**
	You can target enemies by using "TAB" which will automatically target nearby enemies on screen, or you can click on them directly.
		You can target allies by clicking on them directly or using the F1-4 keys, see [[Controls & Commands]] for more information

**Melee Combat**
	Melee combat can be activated by pressing "Q" and moving in close to your target. Your character will then automatically begin auto attacking the enemy. Some [[enemies]] may move and reposition themselves depending on their type so you may need to adjust as well.
		Depending on equipment and [[classes|class]] you may be able to perform a Parry, Evasion, or Block, these will completely avoid a melee attack and possibly let you counter with various Combat Styles.

**Combat Styles**
	If your class has access to them you may perform combat styles. Combat Styles are found in the Ability Book by pressing "B". You can move your styles to your hotbar by left clicking on them in the Ability Book, then left clicking on the hotbar where you'd like the skill to be.
		To use a combat style after placing it on your bar. Ensure Auto Attack is enabled and a target selected, then press the combat style. You'll prepare the style as seen in the combat log, then attempt to execute it on your next melee swing.
			Some styles require conditions to be met before they can succeed and need to be performed within the allotted time window, see [[classes]] for more information on styles your class has access to.
				Styles also cost "Endurance", or the yellow resource bar. If you do not have enough to satisfy the cost of the Combat Style you will fail the style.

**Spells & Abilities**
	All classes have access to spells and/or abilities. These are found in the Ability Book by pressing "B". You can move these to your hotbar by left clicking on them in the Ability Book, then left clicking on the hotbar where you'd like the skill to be.
		To use these you must have a target and be within the range allowed for the spell or skill, you must also have the available mana, or the blue resource bar, available.
			You can read more about your spells and abilities on the [[classes]] page.

**Armor & Resistances**
	Armor is broken down to four types. Cloth, Leather, Chain, and Plate. Some [[classes]] are limited on the armor types they can wear. Accessories such as rings and amulets are not typed as such and can be used by anyone.
		Cloth provides Negation
		Leather provides Evasion and Negation
		Chain provides Evasion, Negation, and Absorbtion
		Plate provides Absorbtion and Negation
			Negation directly reduces *physical* damage received by an amount, has diminishing returns after you've obtained so much of it.
			Evasion provides a flat chance to avoid any melee attack, even elemental based ones (though not spells directly)
			Absorbtion reduces *physical* damage by a percentage, has diminishing returns after you've obtained so much of it.
				Ideally you'll want a spread of defenses while staying under the soft caps.
	Armor can also have resistances available on them. Resistances to elements reduce the damage taken on average from spells or attacks of that elemental type.
		Some enemies do perform melee hits with elemental damage, these cannot be reduced by Negation or Absorbtion and must be avoided with Evasion, Parry, and Block, or reduced with resistances.

**Experience & Leveling**
	Each kill will grant experience towards your next level which is represented by the orange resource bar. Once the bar is full, you'll be given the option to level up. In most cases this is safe to do so, but enemies will also scale up with you, so in rare instances you may wish to remain a lower level to farm a specific piece of [[equipment]].
		Max level is currently 20

For more information on what you'll be using these tactics on, see the [[Enemies]] page.
