Before it broke, the world was a
Prism--sevenfold and single.
Each shard was not a piece, but a
facet, turning in harmony: light,
motion, thought, form, time,
dream, breath. No division. No
exile. Then the prism cracked.

Not from within. Not by war or
folly. But from something
beneath all names.

It came not with thunder, but
with stillness. A sudden halt in
the music of being. A silence so
total it split the stars. The shards
fell inward. Some became
places. Others, beings.

Some we live on. Some we are.
And what was lost in the fall?

That is the question every
seeker carries like a wound. Not
what was broken. But what
broke it--and whether it
watches still.
