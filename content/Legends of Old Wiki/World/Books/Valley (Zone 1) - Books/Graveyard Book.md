Various notes within the book
appear to be from different
sources...

The last thing I remember was a
warmth behind me. My hearth.
Then cold. The sea... folding up
into itself. As if it had never
been.
--Yureth, salt-tender of Oslin's
Reach

A gate of glass. I was pulled
through it. Not opened--not
stepped through--pulled. My
daughter was in the next room. I
never heard her call my name.
--No name given

No pain. No sound. Just light
becoming flat. I was a voice 
without a throat for what felt
like centuries. Then fingers--
not mine--reached in and shaped me again.
--Erekas, of the chainbound
chorus

Patterns emerge. A common
cadence:
Sudden rupture. Dislocation.
Absence.
Then retrieval.

The il'Jiktar deny their
involvement. Yet all who return
do so through them.

Their silence is too practiced to
be innocent. Do they know? Do
they? Have they always known?
How could they not? Have you
considered it? Will you? You,
who are reading this, must
know.. yes, you surely must..
