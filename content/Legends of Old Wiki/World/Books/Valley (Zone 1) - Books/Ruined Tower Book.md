I still climb the broken tower
every time I return. The steps
crumble the same way. The
breeze carries the same scent.
Nothing decays here--not truly.
Not anymore.

Above me, the sky remains
fixed: that pale, relentless 
daylight. No dusk. No stars. No
sleep.

This place does not remember
night.

We once gave names to the
constellations--drew meaning
in their movements, their
stories. But here, time resets
with the turning of breath. The
sun hangs, motionless, and the
shadows fall the same with each
arrival.

The il'Jiktar say this is
stabilization. They speak of
continuity as if it were a
kindness.

But without night, the stars
cannot speak.

And without stars, I am no one.
Still, I climb. Still, I watch. Still,
I hope.
That someday, in some
unguarded moment, the sky will
forget itself. And the starts will
remember the names we gave
them.
