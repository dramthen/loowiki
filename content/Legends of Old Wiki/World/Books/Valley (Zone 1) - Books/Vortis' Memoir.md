*Note: This book is found behind the four Ravenous Undead worshiping a pillar inside of the cave system, there is an invisible ramp that leads up through a secret wall containing the book.

Immortality is a consolation
prize for the weak. A desperate
clinging to what should have
been conquered. I had no
interest in preserving a single
life--I wanted more of me. A
multiplication of genius. A
legion of selfhood. The formula
was forbidden, of course. Even
the Scholars burned those pages.
But I remembered enough.

The soul, under the right
pressure, folds like steel. With
precise incision and perfectly
primed blood--my blood,
unborn--I cast the spell not to
divide, but to replicate.

The first was a failure. It sobbed
and curled in on itself, a
slouching embryonic waste.
I ground its spine beneath my
heel.

The second spoke in backwards
tongues and laughed while
dreaming. I peeled its skin to
study the runes it carved into its
own nerves. Then came the
Third.

It did not tremble.

It did not ask why.

It smiled.

For days, it watched me in
silence. Then it began
completing my thoughts aloud.
Casting my spells faster than I
could recall the components. It
wandered the sanctum at night,
humming spells I had not yet
written.

At first I was proud. Then...
threatened. It began to write its
own tomes, inscribe glyphs I
could not read, speak with
things I could not see. It build a
second sanctum, deeper and
colder than mind, it filled it
with artifacts I do not remember
crafting. It knew what I feared
before I named it.

I tried to unmake it, but the
Third anticipated the attempt
and smiled wider still. I saw in
its gaze not reverence, not even
rivalry--just contempt. I had
forged not a servant, not a
sibling, but a superior.

The ritual if nearly complete. A
temporal statis--cleaner than ice,
quieter than death. It must be
done. The Third has grown...
recursive. To capable. Too
aware. I catch it watching me as if
I'm the experiment. Perhaps it is
a flaw in the process. Perhaps not.
Either way, this chamber will
serve as a vault. Soon, he will
remain seated, perfectly still,
preserved at the height of power.
I fear, then, that I must walk
away. Let that be enough.
