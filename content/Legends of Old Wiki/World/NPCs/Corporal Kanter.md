I don't rightly recall the precise
moment fate twisted me from a
simple soldier into something
else entirely. One minute, I was
standing watch over the gates of
Thalanos, the cold wind biting
through my cloak, the next... well,
the world itself seemed to
splinter beneath my feet.

The crack wasn't in the stone
walls or the cobblestones - It was
deeper, something primal in the
very air. A sharp fracture that tore
through reality, swallowing light
and sound whole. One moment, I
was shouting warnings to my
comrades; The gate! The GATE!
Oh, it must be held, lest all that
we had worked for would be
wrought to ruin... The next,
silence. Blackness. Time
stretched thin, endless and
hollow.

Then came the il'Jiktar--
shadowed figures with eyes like
burning embers--dragging me
from the void's drifting grasp.
Why they want us, I do not know.
Perhaps they seek warriors, or
prisoners, or worse. I do not feel a
sense of safety here - I feel the
same panic I felt when reality
itself played its cruel trick... I
have let my brothers and sisters
down. The same is...
unbearable.

But hear me now, follow
wonderer: to be plucked from
oblivion and set on solid ground
again, no matter how strange or
cruel, is a mercy. I am here, I
breathe, and I stand guard still--
not just for my home of
Thalanos, but for all of us caught
between the cracks of a broken world.
