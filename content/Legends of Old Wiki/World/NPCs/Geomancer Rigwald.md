You mistake your arrival for an
accident. That is...
understandable.

The shattering leaves any adrift
--scraps of will floating through
dead seams in the firmament.
Most dissolve. You did not.

That is why we brought you here.

I am Rigwald. I speak for stone,
and for silence. Where others
see cracks in the world, I see
questions--layers beneath
layers. You were buried deep,
between them. I followed the
faultline until I felt you stir.

Why? Hmm. You're not ready
for that answer. Not yet. You
still smell of where you came
from--grit, steel, and grief.
Those who fall from the
fracture rarely remember the
decent, only the absence that
followed.

It's better that way.

You'll want to find reason.
Closure. The one you lost in the
folding. They all do. But reason
is bedrock--it doesn't lie on the
surface. You dig, or you rot.

So dig.

There are others like you now.
Not many. The il'Jiktar don't
gather lightly. But things
beneath this world are shifting
again. And those who remember
what was are few.
